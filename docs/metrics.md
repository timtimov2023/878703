# Metrics Guide

This project uses simple commercial metrics.

## Funnel

### Impressions

How many times the offer, listing, ad, sign, or page was shown.

### Views

How many people opened the listing, page, profile, QR link, or offer.

### Contacts

How many people contacted the business: message, call, form, direct request.

### Deals

How many contacts became paying customers.

## Conversion metrics

### View rate

`views / impressions`

Shows whether the headline, image, location, or first screen attracts attention.

### Contact rate from views

`contacts / views`

Shows whether the offer is clear and strong enough.

### Deal rate from contacts

`deals / contacts`

Shows lead quality and sales process quality.

## Cost metrics

### Cost per contact

`ad_cost / contacts`

Useful for comparing channels.

### Cost per deal

`ad_cost / deals`

More important than cost per contact.

### Marketing payback

`gross_profit / ad_cost`

Shows whether advertising paid back after direct costs.

## Practical interpretation

If impressions are high and views are low, the problem is usually the title, first image, location, targeting, or offer angle.

If views are high and contacts are low, the problem is usually weak offer clarity, weak trust, unclear price, poor proof, or no obvious call to action.

If contacts are high and deals are low, the problem is usually qualification, response speed, pricing, sales script, or mismatch between ad promise and real offer.
